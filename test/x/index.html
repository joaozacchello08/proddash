Corinthians: 2012 Club World Cup Champions

A Historic Victory

In 2012, Sport Club Corinthians Paulista achieved a monumental milestone by winning the FIFA Club World Cup, held in Japan. The Brazilian club, representing South America as the Copa Libertadores champions, defeated Chelsea FC 1-0 in the final on December 16, 2012, at the International Stadium in Yokohama.

The sole goal was scored by Peruvian striker Paolo Guerrero in the 69th minute, heading in a precise cross from teammate Danilo. This victory marked Corinthians' second Club World Cup title, following their 2000 triumph, solidifying their status as a global football powerhouse.

The Road to Glory

  • Semi-Final: Corinthians defeated Al-Ahly SC (Egypt) 1-0, with Guerrero scoring the decisive goal.
  • Final: A tactical masterclass led by coach Tite, with goalkeeper Cássio making crucial saves against Chelsea's star-studded lineup.
  • Key Players: Paolo Guerrero, Cássio, Danilo, and Emerson Sheik were instrumental in the campaign.

Legacy of 2012

The 2012 Club World Cup victory was a defining moment for Corinthians, celebrated by millions of fans, known as the "Fiel." The triumph showcased the club's resilience, tactical discipline, and passionate fanbase. To this day, it remains a proud chapter in Corinthians' storied history, symbolizing their ability to compete and win on the world stage.
